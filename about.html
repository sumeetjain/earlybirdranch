ABOUT US

Shae Lynn Watt (nee Zastrow)
Community and family are what drive me. Whether it was joining the local theater group, volunteering on a farm, tutoring disadvantaged students, or advocating for the rights of Native peoples, I have always been driven by the belief that interdependence is a blessing. Since beginning this adventure with Kevin, I have been awed by the power of sustainable agriculture to rebuild not only our soils, but also our communities. I believe that raising wholesome and delicious food in a transparent way has the power to reinvigorate local economies because it incentivizes the creation of the all-too-elusive small- and medium-sized farm. I wake up inspired by the work I get to do each day and excited that I will get to do it with my husband. Iím so grateful to our customers, because it is only through their support that our commitment to quality and innovation is possible.

Kevin Watt
Farming was never the plan. Throughout my life Iíve worked to become a member of the professional world. During my undergraduate and graduate years in school, I intently pursued my love of teaching and research down the path to becoming a professor. However, over the last few years Iíve found that I am more intellectually stimulated, personally inspired, and generally content as a farmer. The deep, and sometimes daunting, complexity of working cooperatively with nature is a constant puzzle worth solving. I have fallen in love only twice in my life, and both times it happened completely and at first site. The first time was when I met Shae Lynn and knew who I wanted to share the rest of my life with. The second was when I saw how superb and beneficial farms can be, and I knew how I wanted to spend those years.
